Introduction

Kiva expands financial mobility by giving underserved populations the opportunity to access loans through zero-interest crowdfunding. By creating a platform that allows leendees to partner with lenders, Kiva scales the scope of capital to let it do what money does best: create wealth.

At its current stage, access to loans can be daunting at best and a barrier to the productivity and success of livelihoods at worst. When underserved populations can work to give themselves necessary resources to contribute to bettering their lives and participating in the economy, everyone is better off. In addition to having a direct impact on the lives of those moving up the socio-economic ladder, the creation of wealth improves the lives of humanity as a whole. Our group and Kiva both share the same vision: that financial mobility helps humans flourish. Thereby our strong belief in expanded access to capital and the creation of wealth inspired us to support Kiva and motivated us to do our best work.

Research

  • Competitor Analysis

    Before we started working on the user research, our team completed a competitor analysis. On the screen you are going to see 4 different companies, 2 of them are direct competitors and two of them are indirect competitors. Zadisha and United Prosperity were microloaning companies, while gofundme and kickstarter are fundraising companies.

  • Interview

    Once we narrowed down concepts and features that we wanted to incorporate based on our competitor analysis, we conducted interviews. As a user researcher I wanted to learn how lendees are motivated to donate and lend to their local and greater community so that more targeted engagement could be fostered.

  • Survey

    Along with our interview, we also sent our surveys. These surveys aimed to uncover the demographics and behavior trends of those who donate. We distilled this information to cater more specifically to those who want to participate in services such as Kiva.

Problem

We believe creating a relationship-driven platform for lenders and borrowers alike will build trust to establish a unified force, enabling underserved communities to fulfill greater financial success. We will know this is true when Kiva's expands its regular lender base.

Iteration

Version 1
We knew we wanted a playful and earthy design for our Splash Screen. Our intents behind this idea were to create a sense of connection and warmth for our users. Our initial hesitation was that this design confilicted with the minimalistic style we had in mind.

Version 1
During our user research phase, our team discovered that when it comes to donations and lending users appreciate visual indicators of a campaign's progress. With this idea in mind, we also made sure to prototype a campaign page that would showcase popular projects being donated to.

Version 1
Our team noticed that an under-utilized feature amongst Kiva's competitors is object oriented design. We decided that to keep within our theme of community, we wanted to present the different catergories that lendees could pick through compelling visuals. However, we struggled to convey powerful imagery within the confines of a tile.
Splash Screen

Through multiple iterations, our group finalized our Splash Screen by blending simple solid colors with a textured topical background to symbolize a global community. We carefully chose the warm green and muted pink to prime our users with the ideas of giving.
Campaign Page

After testing out our initial designs, our testers expressed feeling overwhelmed by too much information. As a result, we simplified our progress bar with playful icorns and minimized content within "Learn More" tiles.
Categories

Our team decided to pair leveraging the power of images with minimalistic design. We found that incorporating small pictures of those seeking funding devalued their stories... so instead, we utilized fun icons.
